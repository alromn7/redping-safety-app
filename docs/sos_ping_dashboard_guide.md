# SOS Ping Dashboard for SAR Members

**Last Updated: December 20, 2024**  
**Version: 1.0**

## Overview

The SOS Ping Dashboard is a dedicated interface for SAR (Search and Rescue) members to view, respond to, and coordinate emergency rescues. When users trigger SOS alerts, they appear as "pings" on this dashboard, allowing SAR teams to efficiently assess and respond to emergencies based on their capabilities and location.

## Using the Dashboard

### 1. **Viewing Active Emergencies**

The **Active** tab shows all current emergencies in your area:

- **Red priority bar**: Indicates emergency priority level
- **User avatar**: Shows person's initials
- **Time elapsed**: How long since the SOS was triggered
- **Distance**: Your distance from the emergency location
- **Emergency type icon**: Visual indicator of emergency type
- **Medical indicators**: Blood type, conditions, allergies
- **Risk and accessibility levels**: Rescue difficulty assessment

### 2. **Responding to Emergencies**

#### Quick Response
- **"Unavailable"** button: Mark yourself as unavailable for this emergency
- **"Respond"** button: Accept the emergency and get assigned

#### Detailed Response
- Tap on any emergency card to open detailed information
- **Details Tab**: Complete emergency and person information
- **Respond Tab**: Set response parameters:
  - Custom message to victim
  - Estimated arrival time
  - Vehicle type (Car, ATV, Helicopter, Boat, etc.)
  - Available equipment selection
  - Team member coordination

#### Response Information
When responding, you can specify:
- **Equipment available**: First Aid Kit, AED, Oxygen, Stretcher, etc.
- **Vehicle type**: Ground vehicle, ATV, helicopter, boat, on foot
- **Estimated arrival**: 5-120 minutes
- **Team members**: If responding as a team
- **Message**: Reassurance or instructions for the victim

### 3. **Managing Assigned Rescues**

The **Assigned** tab shows your active rescue assignments:

#### Status Updates
- **"En Route"**: Mark when you're traveling to the location
- **"On Scene"**: Mark when you arrive at the emergency
- **"Complete"**: Mark the rescue as completed

#### Detailed Operations
Access full rescue management through the **Actions** tab:
- **Status updates** with optional messages
- **Backup requests** for additional SAR teams
- **Medical escalation** to emergency medical services
- **Completion notes** for rescue documentation

### 4. **Filtering and Search**

Use the filter button (üîç) to customize your view:

#### Distance Filter
- Adjust the radius from 1km to 200km
- Shows only emergencies within your selected range

#### Priority Filters
- **Low Priority**: Non-urgent situations
- **Medium Priority**: Standard emergency response
- **High Priority**: Urgent medical attention needed
- **Critical Priority**: Life-threatening emergency

#### Risk Level Filters
- **Low Risk**: Minimal danger to rescuer
- **Medium Risk**: Standard rescue precautions
- **High Risk**: Elevated danger, extra caution needed
- **Critical Risk**: Extreme danger, specialized team required

## Emergency Information Details

### Personal Information
- **Name and contact**: Victim identification
- **Age and medical**: Critical health information
- **Blood type**: For medical emergencies
- **Allergies**: Important for medication administration
- **Medical conditions**: Existing health issues

### Location Details
- **GPS coordinates**: Precise location data
- **Address**: Human-readable location
- **Accessibility level**: Rescue difficulty (Easy, Moderate, Difficult, Extreme)
- **Terrain type**: Environmental conditions
- **Weather conditions**: Current weather at location

### Emergency Context
- **SOS type**: How the emergency was triggered
- **Impact information**: For crash/fall detection
- **User message**: Direct communication from victim
- **Time elapsed**: Duration since emergency started
- **Required equipment**: Suggested rescue equipment

## Response Workflow

### 1. **Emergency Detection**
- User triggers SOS (manual, crash detection, fall detection)
- System creates SOS ping with all available information
- Ping appears on all nearby SAR member dashboards

### 2. **SAR Assessment**
- SAR members review emergency details
- Assess their capability to respond
- Consider distance, equipment, and team availability

### 3. **Response Assignment**
- SAR member accepts the emergency
- System assigns the rescue to that member
- Victim is notified of incoming help

### 4. **Rescue Coordination**
- SAR member updates status as they progress
- Victim receives real-time updates
- Additional resources can be requested if needed

### 5. **Completion**
- SAR member marks rescue as completed
- Completion notes are recorded
- Emergency is closed in the system

## Best Practices

### For SAR Members
- **Check the dashboard regularly** for new emergencies
- **Respond quickly** to critical and high-priority alerts
- **Provide accurate arrival estimates** to help victims
- **Update your status** throughout the rescue operation
- **Use clear, reassuring messages** when communicating with victims
- **Request backup early** if the situation exceeds your capabilities

### Response Priorities
1. **Life-threatening emergencies** (Critical priority)
2. **Medical emergencies** (High priority)
3. **Injury situations** (Medium priority)
4. **Non-medical emergencies** (Low priority)

### Equipment Considerations
- **Always carry basic first aid** equipment
- **Coordinate with team members** for specialized equipment
- **Consider terrain and weather** when selecting equipment
- **Communicate equipment limitations** to dispatch

## Safety Guidelines

- **Never exceed your training level** or capabilities
- **Request backup** for dangerous or complex rescues
- **Follow established SAR protocols** and procedures
- **Maintain communication** with dispatch and team members
- **Document incidents properly** for learning and legal purposes

## Technical Requirements

- **Verified SAR member status** required for access
- **Location services** enabled for distance calculations
- **Stable internet connection** for real-time updates
- **Device permissions** for notifications and location
